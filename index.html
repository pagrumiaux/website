I am currently a PhD student in audio and artificial intelligence, in a collaboration between Orange Labs and GIPSA-lab. I am in the audio research team within Orange Labs, and in the CRISSP team at GIPSA-lab.

My PhD research focuses on the problem of audio source localization and tracking, specifically on speech signals. I am investigating the possibilities of using deep learning on 3D audio signals to improve the existing models.

My other research interests include music information retrieval and music generation.

In addition to research, playing and composing music take up a lot of space in my daily life. I have been playing the piano since I am 6, and I like playing pieces in different styles like classical music, blues, boogie-woogie or minimal music. I also like playing by ear and rearranging songs I like.

I also spend a lot of time composing music using synthesizers and a DAW (Ableton Live in my case). I create tracks in several genres I love, including dub, electronic, ambient, psytrance, rap beats and other styles I don't know how to classify.

I also like doing sound design by messing around with synthesizers, such as Arturia's Microfreak, Arturia's Pigments (yes I like Arturia's products!) or Novation's Bass Station II. I sometimes record sounds from the world with a microphone, either to process and use them in my creations, or for nothing but pleasure.
